Company Process Guide

Training modules built from the first 15 DOCX files. Each section teaches a skill so a new contributor can plug into the Austin Visuals production engine without shadowing.
Objective

Take one pain-point pillar and turn it into a 30–45 day sequence across Matt’s LinkedIn, the company LinkedIn, Facebook, YouTube clips, and Google Business without sounding recycled.

1
Source material & tone pack Nancy David
Pull: latest approved blog, positioning memo, asset folder, and any legacy post you plan to reuse. Highlight the buyer pain and the city/industry angle. Practice drill: rewrite one 2022 post and compare to the new tone (journalistic, calm authority).
2
Calendar build
Deliverable: 30-row sheet. Columns = Date, Channel, Hook, Asset, CTA, Status, Approver. Rotate industries (oil & gas, medical device, legal) and objectives (clarity, speed, AI assist). Tip: calendars are execution tools, not law—stamp each with “Campaign window + owner.”
3
Rewrite for reuse
If you reuse archive content, change hook, CTA, and time references. Use the “three edits” test: new lead sentence, updated stat/example, refreshed CTA. Spot-check yourself by reading aloud—anything that sounds dated gets rewired.
4
Asset pairing & delivery
Attach a render, still, or reel clip to every slot. Use the legal/medical template when required. Upload finished copy + assets to the scheduler (MeetEdgar/Buffer/Hootsuite — confirm the current platform before your run). If the tool lacks a slot, fill the manual tracker and flag Tony.
5
Google Business shadow set
For each pillar blog, write a 120–150 word Google Business post with local cues (“Houston med device teams”, “Austin robotics”). Include keyword, CTA, and internal link. Schedule in sync with the blog drop.
6
Quality checks & handoff
Before hand-off to Matt/Nancy: run the “Staleness Scan” (no dated jokes, no reused timestamp). Confirm contact block, CTA, and asset links. Save the full pack in Dropbox ➜ Marketing ➜ Calendars ➜ YYYY-MM-DD. Tag Tony to archive.
  • ✅ Cadence conflicts logged (if doc vs real cadence disagree)
  • ✅ Tool used noted in tracker (MeetEdgar / Buffer / Hootsuite)
  • ✅ Reuse reason + edits documented
Objective

Draft, proof, and stage a value-first newsletter that adds new intel beyond the source blog, hits the Wednesday night / Thursday AM cadence, and produces downstream snippets.

1
Subject line workshop Nancy
Write five options. Requirements: ties to money/status/speed, under 55 characters, no clickbait. Share with Matt for pick + angle confirmation before writing.
2
Value body + proof pack
Outline intro → two proof blocks → CTA. Proof block = stat, example, or cost tip not found in the blog. Pull from case studies, pricing sheets, or SME quotes. Call out the persona (marketing director, owner, product lead) explicitly.
3
Manual build
Paste into Mailchimp template manually (automation not approved). Double-check buttons, alt text, and text-to-image ratio. Save a PDF proof + HTML export in Dropbox ➜ Marketing ➜ Newsletters.
4
Approval and downstream assets
Send draft + PDF to Matt for green light. After approval, carve two LinkedIn posts and one Google Business snippet from the same content. Note cadence variance (monthly vs bi-monthly) if you had to adjust.
  • ✅ Value before pitch (no “Austin Visuals is amazing” in paragraph one)
  • ✅ Quant proof present (chart, number, or cost comparison)
  • ✅ Manual Mailchimp insert logged with timestamp

Objective

Run a discovery call that positions Austin Visuals as the specialist, qualifies the lead, and gathers every input production will need.

1
Positioning primer Matt Sales
Memorize the distinctions: technical accuracy, multi-style capability, direct specialist vs agency middle-man. Practice the 60-second “why Austin Visuals” script before every call.
2
Qualification grid
Capture BANT + Input readiness. Use the intake form: role, audience, why now, budget comfort, timeline, assets on hand (script, runtime, brand kit, CAD/photos), end use (trade show, investor, regulator, training). Missing inputs = risk callout.
3
Discovery playback
Summarize back to the prospect: “You’re a marketing director serving X audience, you need clarity for Y, your internal assets include…”. Ask, “What did I miss?” to avoid hidden stakeholders later.
4
Handoff kit
Drop call recording + filled intake form + asset links into Dropbox ➜ Client ➜ Client Reference. Notify Nancy + assigned PM. Do not move to proposal until assets are confirmed or paid concept step is ordered.
  • ✅ Intake form stored with date + initials
  • ✅ Missing assets flagged (script/brand/CAD)
  • ✅ Opportunity tagged as “Ready / Needs Starter / Hold”
Objective

Turn hesitation into a smaller paid step and reintroduce Austin Visuals to past clients without sounding dated.

1
Diagnose the block
Ask: Is the vision unclear? Are stakeholders unconvinced? Is budget/timeline fuzzy? Document which bucket the objection belongs to before offering solutions.
2
Offer a low-friction starter
Menu: Concept sketch pack (3-day), Storyboard sprint (1 week), or Look-dev frame. Price each so it feels attainable. Explain that deliverables roll forward if they green-light full production.
3
Reactivation script refresh
Use the legacy structure (relationship-first, “seeing what you’re working on”). Update language to today’s offerings (AI-assisted clarity labs, end-to-end visualization). Log outcomes in CRM immediately.
4
Next-step lock-in
End every objection or reactivation call with a dated next action: starter package invoice, follow-up after stakeholder review, or closed-lost reason. No “we’ll circle back.”
  • ✅ Starter offer documented in CRM
  • ✅ Reactivation tone updated (no legacy slang)
  • ✅ Next step + owner recorded
Objective

Gather the full intake package before production starts so costs stay down and revisions stay low.

1
Seven-folder setup Tony PM
Create Client / Art / Client Reference / Project Files / Renders / Scripts / Uploads. Drop all inbound materials into Uploads first; Tony routes to the right subfolder after tagging.
2
Minimum viable kit
Need before quote locks: script or outline, target runtime, brand kit (logo, fonts, colors), visual references, objective + audience statement. Use the “missing input” template email if something is absent.
3
Technical add-ons
For industrial/medical/regulatory work collect CAD files, engineering drawings, field photos, or SME availability. Flag these in the intake form so art knows where accuracy is critical.
4
Revision guardrails
Set expectations: two structured rounds included. Log every note in the tracker, confirm receipt with the client, and tie each change back to the original objectives to prevent scope creep.
5
Archive + reuse
After final delivery (and last 25% payment), move masters to Project Files + Uploads ➜ Client. Tag deliverables with industry + permissions so marketing can reuse safely.
  • ✅ Intake sheet saved in Client Reference
  • ✅ Technical assets verified (CAD/drawings if needed)
  • ✅ Revision limits communicated in writing
